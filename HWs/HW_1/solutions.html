ENCRYPTION

Or rather, Decryption. Quantum cryptography has its place, but a more popular and thrilling topic is to talk about the threat of a quantum computer decrypting all our currently secure data. RSA encryption has been a standard for secure data transmission, taking advantage of extremely large prime numbers and factorization of said primes. A classical computer would have to brute force these factors, as there is no current solution or algorithm for these massively large numbers. A fun fact here: the largest publicly known RSA number had 829 bits, and took over 2,700 CPU years to crack. Most RSA keys are typically 1.5 to 4 times larger than this record. Therefore, quantum computing magic comes in, and if leveraged correctly, would be able to factor large RSA keys in polynomial time using Shor's algorithm. For a standard 2048-bit RSA key, it could take millions or billions of years for a classical computer to crack! Astronimically unbelievable!

Back in 1994,(3 years before I was even born), Peter Shor developed a quantum algorithm to be able to factor large prime numbers. This algorithm assumes the quantum computer has enough qubits, and minimal, maybe zero, need for error correction. This algorithm is known as Shor's algorithm, and is the key to cracking RSA encryption, but don't worry! We are very very far aware from developing a quantum computer that has these "perfect" scenarios for Shor's algorithm to work, so all our data is still safe for the forseeable future.
